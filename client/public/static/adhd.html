ADHD

Introduction:

Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by a persistent pattern of inattention and/or hyperactivity-impulsivity that interferes with functioning or development.

Symptoms:

ADHD symptoms can vary, but they generally fall into two categories:

  1. Inattention:
    • Difficulty sustaining attention in tasks or play activities.
    • Frequent careless mistakes in schoolwork or other activities.
    • Often does not seem to listen when spoken to directly.
    • Difficulty organizing tasks and activities.
    • Frequently loses items necessary for tasks or activities.
  2. Hyperactivity and Impulsivity:
    • Often fidgets with or taps hands or feet, or squirms in seat.
    • Frequently leaves seat in situations where remaining seated is expected.
    • Runs about or climbs in inappropriate situations.
    • Often talks excessively.
    • Interrupts or intrudes on others (e.g., butts into conversations or games).

Management Tips:

  1. Structured Environment:
    • Create a consistent daily routine to help manage symptoms.
    • Organize tasks with checklists and visual aids.
  2. Behavioral Therapy:
    • Work with a therapist to develop strategies for managing symptoms.
  3. Medication:
    • Consult with a healthcare provider about medication options that may help manage ADHD symptoms.
  4. Healthy Lifestyle:
    • Encourage regular physical activity and a balanced diet.
  5. Support Systems:
    • Seek support from family, friends, and ADHD support groups.
